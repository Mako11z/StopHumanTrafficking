What is Human Trafficking?

Each year millions of men, woman and children are forced and sold into situations where they are beaten, sexually exploited, manipulated or forced into doing some kind of labor under terrible conditions.


Human trafficking is the act of manipulating or forcing a person into performing sexual acts or services with the aim of using that person for profit and exploitaion.

Who are the Victims?

Trafficking happens all over the world in all sociteties and commnities. It happens to anyone of any age, any gender and any race or nationality. A victum could be a man, a woman forced into prostitution or even a young child who doesnt know how to get help.


Traffickers will target and deceive in any way possible to get their hands on their target. Traffikcers control and abuse thier victums in ways where it leads to long term mental and emotional health issues such as: guilt,post traumatic stress disorder,depression,anxiety,substance abuse

How it Happens?

Traffickers come from all different backgrounds and ethnicities. They can be a neihbor, a close friend, or even a family member but in all scenarios, traffickers will do just about anything to lure their vixtums into thier grasp.


Traffikcers use a variety of coercive and manipulative methods such as using false promise of job opportunities or get rich quick, withholding travel documents or anything keeping that person stuck in that area, posting on a website offering work, offering emotional support and love, applying threats to the indivisual or to family members and close friends, kidnapping or physically pushed into the situation

Statistics

In 2021, there were over 10,000 cases of human trafficking were reported to the United States National Human Trafficking Hotline

Roughly 400,000 cases reported to the hotline since its launch in 2007

Only one out of one hundred human trafficking victims are rescued

It's estimated that about only 0.04% of human trafficking cases are identified, meaning 99.06% of cases are undetected.

The International Labour Organization estimated that there were over 24 million victims of Human Traficking in 2016

4.8 Million of them being sex trafficking victums

70% of human trafficking victums are females with 90% of the cases involving sexual exploitation

Trafficking victims have an average life exspencency of about 7 to 10 years
